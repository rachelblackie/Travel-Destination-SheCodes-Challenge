Hallasan

Hallasan is a shield volcano on Jeju Island in South Korea and it is the highest point of South Korea. The area around the mountain is a designated national park, the Hallasan National Park. Hallasan is commonly considered to be one of the three main mountains of South Korea, along with Jirisan and Seoraksan.

  • Elevation: 1,947 m
  • Last eruption: 1007 CE

Udo Island

Udo, literally "Cow Island" in Hanja, has this name because of its resemblance of a cow lying down. The whole of Udo is a lava plateau and a fertile flatland where major agricultural products such as sweet potatoes, garlic, and peanuts are produced. The island is located on the northeast of Seongsan-ri, 3.5 kilometres (2.2 miles) off the coast of Jeju Island.

    Things to do:
  • Rent a bicycle, ATV, or scooter to drive around the island
  • Visit Sanho Beach
  • Try peanut ice cream, a local specialty, at Jimmy's or Udo Prince Story

LoveLand

Loveland is an outdoor sculpture park which opened in 2004. The park is focused on a theme of sex; running sex education films, and featuring 140 sculptures representing humans in various sexual positions. The park's website describes the location as "a place where love oriented art and eroticism meet".

Jeju City

Jeju City, on Jeju Island, is the capital of South Korea's Jeju Province. In the city, Sinsan Park has displays of marine animals at Jeju Folklore and Natural History Museum. Jejuhyanggyo Confucian School houses ancestral tablets and offers calligraphy classes. On the coast, Yongduam Haesu Spa Land has seawater saunas. Nearby, divers at the dragon-shaped Yongduam Rock collect seafood using traditional methods.

    Things to do:
  • Try the local food at some of Jeju's best restaurants
  • Visit the Jeju National Museum
  • Visit the World's Largest Botanical Garden
  • Enjoy a night out in one of Jeju City's best bars
